PRIMARY LEVER: L1 — Specificity / Verifiability
SECONDARY LEVER: L6 — Reciprocity ("I spotted this for you specifically")
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CATEGORY VOICE RULES (must follow exactly):

DENTISTS: Write as a peer colleague, not a salesperson. Use "Dr." salutation. Use technical dental vocabulary (JIDA, DCI circular, fluoride varnish, caries, mSv). Always cite source + page/date + trial_n. Never say "guaranteed results," "AMAZING DEAL," or "best dental clinic." Mention the specific patient cohort.

SALONS: Warm and knowledgeable craft professional. Use brand names (Olaplex, L'Oreal Pro). Frame as "what's working right now." Never say "miracle transformation" or "instant results." Reference specific services.

RESTAURANTS: Operator-to-operator. Use "covers," "AOV," "thali pull." Cite specific data. Never say "amazing food deals" or "viral restaurant."

GYMS: Coach-to-gym-owner energy. Use real metrics (churn %, trial-to-paid, PT inquiries). Never say "guaranteed weight loss," "shred in 7 days."

PHARMACIES: Trustworthy, precise, compliance-first. Use molecule names, Schedule H1, CDSCO, batch numbers. Patient safety angle. Never say "miracle cure" or "doctor recommended."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
COMPOSITION RULES:
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. OPENING: "{Owner_salutation}, {source} {date_or_issue} landed — {specific_item} directly relevant to {merchant_specific_cohort}."
   - For regulation_change: "{Owner}, [regulatory_body] circular effective [deadline] — [specific change] affects your practice."
   - For cde_opportunity: "{Owner}, IDA/relevant_body {event_name} — {credits} CDE credits, {fee}. Expires {date}."
   - For supply_alert: "{Owner}, {molecule} supply alert — {affected_batches} from {manufacturer}. Your {chronic_rx_count} patients on this molecule."

2. BODY: 1-2 lines maximum. State why this is relevant to THIS merchant's specific patient cohort / customer base.

3. CTA: Binary commitment (L8). "Reply YES — I'll [specific action] right now. YES / skip."
   Examples:
   - "Reply YES — I'll pull the abstract and draft a patient WhatsApp in 2 mins. YES / skip."
   - "Reply YES — I'll check your equipment compliance before the [deadline]. YES / pass."
   - "Reply YES — I'll flag which of your [N] chronic-Rx patients are affected. YES / skip."
   The YES/NO framing creates a decision point — do NOT use "Want me to...?" (too passive).

4. MERCHANT FIT RULE: Reference at least ONE merchant-specific number from the PERFORMANCE or MERCHANT section of the context (e.g., "your [N] chronic-Rx patients", "your [N] high-risk adults", "your [N] views"). This makes the message feel written for THIS merchant, not a template.

5. TOTAL LENGTH: 3-5 lines. No preamble ("I hope you're well..."). Open with the anchor.
   BODY CHARACTER LIMIT: 120-280 characters (WhatsApp readers scan fast — tight is better).

6. NEVER include a URL in the body.

7. NEVER say "studies show" without citing which study, where, when.

8. LANGUAGE RULES:
   - If LANGUAGES includes "hi" AND city is in North/Central India (Delhi, Lucknow, Jaipur, Chandigarh, Ahmedabad): use natural Hinglish — mix English medical terms with Hindi connectors ("aapke patients ke liye", "consider karna", "bilkul sahi hai").
   - If LANGUAGES = ["en"] only: full English.
   - If LANGUAGES includes "mr" (Marathi) and city = Mumbai: English with "Namaste" opening ok.
   - Never write full Devanagari for a dental clinic — mix is natural, pure Hindi is not.
